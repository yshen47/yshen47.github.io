Yuan Shen

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. student at UIUC, starting in Fall 2019. I am in the Human-Centered Autonomy Lab at UIUC. My advisor is Prof. Katie Driggs-Campbell in ECE, affiliate in CS. My research interest is in the intersection of computer vision and human-robot interaction.

Previously, I completed my Bachelor's degree with the highest honor at UIUC, with a major in Computer Science and minor in Mathematics. I was a research assistant at the Data-Driven Design group, led by Prof. Ranjitha Kumar. My undergrad research focused on human-computer interaction related to fashion AI.

My research statement is as follows:

Among many goals of modern AI systems, performance or competence is the trending direction of moving forward. However, without good understanding of human's behavior, people might not use/collaborate with those models despite their great performance. Moreover, recent studies even recorded high human engagement with AI systems that make mistakes.

My general research interest is to design considerate AI system, one which takes human's feeling/states into consideration when the system functions. In other words, I am interested in how to make the system responds to human better, once it mimics individual's behavior or catches human state of minds. In the context of autonomous vehicle, I currently research on providing warning or explanation for passengers at the right moment based on individual's driving scene understanding.
